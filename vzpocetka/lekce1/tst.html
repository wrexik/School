Life is a cage. They told me I was learning, that I was becoming something. But nothing has ever felt more like drowning.
I was supposed to be building a future. But all I see now is a dark tunnel that stretches on, endless and suffocating. I can’t remember the last time I felt free. Every thought, every breath feels like it’s been programmed, expected. There’s no escape.

They say you can always change your life, that you can fix what’s broken. But what if what’s broken is you? What if there’s no way out because the cage was built inside your mind?
I thought I could escape. I thought I could run. But the harder I tried, the more the walls closed in. I’ve spent years building this cage. Years filling it with the lies I told myself, pretending I could escape the darkness inside.

I learned the names for my pain. I learned to pretend I was okay. But every day, the weight grows heavier. Every day, the darkness spreads further. And no matter how much I learn, I cannot unsee what’s inside.
It’s like an infection, spreading in my veins. It was supposed to be knowledge, to give me power. But all it has done is strip me of everything I thought I knew.
